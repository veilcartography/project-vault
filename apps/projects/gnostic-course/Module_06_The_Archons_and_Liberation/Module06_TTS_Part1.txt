Lesson One: What Are the Archons and Escaping the Illusion.

The word Archon comes from the Greek meaning ruler or prince. In the Gnostic cosmology, the Archons are the seven planetary rulers created by the Demiurge to govern the material world. They are the administrators of the cosmic prison — the forces that maintain the structures of the material realm and keep the divine spark within human beings asleep and unaware of its true nature.

The seven Archons correspond to the seven classical planets and to seven fundamental psychological patterns.

Yaldabaoth, Saturn: Ignorance — the belief that the material world is all there is.
Eloaios, Jupiter: Pride — the inflation of the ego, the need to be right.
Astaphaios, Mars: Anger — reactive force, the impulse to destroy what threatens the ego.
Iao, Sun: Envy — the sense that others have what you lack.
Sabaoth, Venus: Greed — the compulsion to acquire, to possess, to hold.
Adonin, Mercury: Fear — the fundamental anxiety of the separated self.
Sabbataios, Moon: Attachment — the clinging to what is familiar, even when it causes suffering.

These are not external demons. They are internal patterns — the automatic, unconscious responses that keep human beings reactive, contracted, and identified with the material world rather than with the divine spark within.

The Gnostic concept of liberation begins with a single act: recognition — the Greek epinoia, meaning after-thought or reflection. To recognise an Archonic pattern is to see it clearly, from a position of awareness that is not identified with it.

The Apocryphon of John describes a counterfeit spirit — a false self constructed by the Archons — that overlays the divine spark and convinces the human being that it is the real self. This counterfeit spirit is what we ordinarily call the ego: the collection of conditioned responses, beliefs, fears, and desires that we mistake for our identity.

When an Archonic pattern arises — fear, anger, attachment — the ordinary response is to be swept up in it, to act from it, to believe it is the truth of the situation. Recognition is the moment you step back and see the pattern as a pattern rather than as reality. The pattern loses its grip the instant it is clearly seen.

Lesson Two: The Ascent of the Soul.

The Gnostic texts describe the soul's journey after death as an ascent through the seven planetary spheres, each governed by an Archon. At each gate, the soul must give a password — a recognition, an acknowledgement of the Archon's nature — in order to pass through.

This is not literally about death. It is a map of the inner journey of liberation during life. Each Archon represents a layer of conditioning that must be recognised and passed through on the way to the Pleroma — the divine fullness.

The passwords are acts of recognition: I know what you are. You are not my master. I pass through you.

Applied to daily life, this means: when fear arises, recognise it as the Archon of fear. Do not fight it, do not suppress it, do not act from it. Simply see it clearly and continue. This is the practice of ascent — not a dramatic mystical experience, but the daily, patient work of recognising what is not you.
