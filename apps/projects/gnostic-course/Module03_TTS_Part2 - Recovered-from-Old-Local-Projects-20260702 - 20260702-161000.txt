Lesson Three: The Apocryphon of John and Other Key Texts.

The Apocryphon of John — the Secret Book of John — is the most complete account of Gnostic cosmology in the Nag Hammadi Library. It is structured as a post-resurrection dialogue between the risen Christ and the apostle John, in which Christ reveals the true nature of reality: the Pleroma, the fall of Sophia, the creation of the Demiurge, the making of the human being, and the path of return.

The description of the Monad — the highest divine principle — reads: He is not corporeal, he is not incorporeal. He is not large, he is not small. He is not someone among other beings, rather he is far superior. This is apophatic theology: defining the divine by what it is not.

Thunder, Perfect Mind is a text unlike any other in the library. It is a poem in the voice of a divine feminine figure who defies all categories: I am the first and the last. I am the honoured one and the scorned one. I am the whore and the holy one. I am the wife and the virgin. I am the mother and the daughter.

This text is often read as the voice of Sophia herself — or of the divine feminine principle that underlies all apparent contradictions. It is best read aloud, slowly, as a meditation.

Practice suggestion: read one passage from Thunder, Perfect Mind aloud each morning for a week. Notice how your relationship to the text changes as you become familiar with its rhythm.
