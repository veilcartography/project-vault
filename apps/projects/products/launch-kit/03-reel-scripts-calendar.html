Reel 1 / Module 1 What is Gnosis? (It's not what you think)
🎣 Hook (first 3 seconds)

Most people think Gnosticism is a religion. It's not. It's the opposite.

⚡ Disrupt (the unexpected angle)

Religion asks you to believe. Gnosis asks you to *know* — through direct experience. Not faith. Not dogma. Experience. The word gnosis is Greek. It means knowledge that changes you. The kind that lands in the body. The kind that makes you say: I didn't read that. I *felt* it.

💡 Insight (the takeaway)

The Gnostics were persecuted not because their ideas were dangerous — but because they taught that you don't need a priest to reach the divine. You are the way in.

Reel 2 / Module 2 You have a divine spark inside you — here's the Gnostic proof
🎣 Hook (first 3 seconds)

You were not born broken. You were born *incomplete*. There's a difference.

⚡ Disrupt (the unexpected angle)

The Gnostics described the Pleroma — a realm of perfect divine light. And they said: a fragment of that light fell into matter. Into you. The divine spark (*pneuma*) is in every human being. Hidden under layers of conditioning, fear, unconscious pattern. The whole spiritual path? One thing: remember what you are.

💡 Insight (the takeaway)

The Demiurge — the creator of the material world in Gnostic cosmology — isn't evil. Just blind. He doesn't know there's something higher. That's why the world feels like it was made by someone who didn't quite understand love.

Reel 3 / Module 3 They tried to destroy these texts. One farmer saved them.
🎣 Hook (first 3 seconds)

In 367 AD, a letter went out ordering the destruction of certain texts. Someone buried 52 of them in a jar in the Egyptian desert.

⚡ Disrupt (the unexpected angle)

They were found in 1945 by a farmer digging for fertiliser near Nag Hammadi. Inside: the Gospel of Thomas. The Apocryphon of John. Thunder, Perfect Mind. 52 texts the Church tried to erase. And the reason they tried? One saying from Thomas, Saying 3: *'The Kingdom is within you and it is outside you.'* You don't need the institution. You are the temple.

💡 Insight (the takeaway)

The Gospel of Thomas has no miracles, no resurrection narrative, no theology. Just 114 sayings of Jesus — raw, strange, deeply interior. Scholars think it may be older than the Gospel of Mark.

Reel 4 / Module 4 Sophia fell so you could wake up — the myth that changes everything
🎣 Hook (first 3 seconds)

She was whole. Perfect. Divine. And she made a choice that broke the cosmos — and saved it.

⚡ Disrupt (the unexpected angle)

Sophia — the divine feminine wisdom — desired to know the Father without her partner. In that reaching, she fell. And from her fall, the material world was born. Most traditions frame this as a mistake. A sin. The Gnostics said: *it was the engine of liberation.* She descended so the divine spark could enter matter. So you could exist. So gnosis could happen *inside* the world.

💡 Insight (the takeaway)

Sophia is not just mythology. She's an archetype of your own wisdom faculty — the inner knowing you've been taught to doubt. Every time you trust your intuition over the voice that says 'be rational' — that's Sophia.

Reel 5 / Module 5 This is why you can study Gnosticism for years and never have gnosis
🎣 Hook (first 3 seconds)

You can understand gnosis completely and never experience it. This is the trap.

⚡ Disrupt (the unexpected angle)

Gnosis is not intellectual. The Gnostics had a word for the faculty that actually accesses it: *nous*. Not your thinking mind. Not your reasoning. The eye of the heart. The faculty that perceives directly — without filter. You've used it. The moments when you *knew* something without being able to explain how. That was nous. The question is: can you turn it on deliberately?

💡 Insight (the takeaway)

The answer is yes. And it requires practice — not more studying. Meditation. Self-inquiry. Sitting with a sacred saying until it speaks, not just to your mind but through your body.

Reel 6 / Module 6 The Archons are not demons — they're the patterns keeping you asleep
🎣 Hook (first 3 seconds)

Fear. Attachment. Anger. Pride. The Gnostics mapped all of them 2,000 years ago and called them *Archons*.

⚡ Disrupt (the unexpected angle)

Not external beings. Not supernatural enemies. Patterns. Architectural features of the unconscious mind that keep human beings trapped in repetitive cycles. The Gnostic map of seven Archons lines up almost exactly with what modern psychology calls the seven core wounds: shame, fear, anger, grief, abandonment, helplessness, confusion. They knew. They just had different words.

💡 Insight (the takeaway)

The path out isn't fighting them. It's *recognition* — the Greek word is *epinoia*. You look at the pattern and say: I see you. You are not me. That moment of clear seeing is what breaks the cycle. Not willpower. Recognition.

🔄 Open Loop (leave them wanting more)

Reel 7 / Module 7 What a Gnostic morning actually looks like
🎣 Hook (first 3 seconds)

Gnosis without practice is just philosophy. Here's what embodied Gnostic practice looks like.

⚡ Disrupt (the unexpected angle)

Morning. Before your phone. Before the news. Light a candle — a physical act that says: this time is different. Open a text — one saying from the Gospel of Thomas. Just one. Sit with it for five minutes. Don't analyse it. Let it work on you. Breathe. Notice what's here. That's it. That's a Gnostic morning. You don't need robes, or a temple, or an initiatory lineage. You need attention and intention.

💡 Insight (the takeaway)

The Bridal Chamber was the Gnostic sacrament of inner union — the masculine and feminine principles within you meeting and becoming whole. This is what the Gospel of Philip describes. It's not about gender — it's about integration.

Reel 8 / Module 8 The Gnostic path doesn't end — here's where it actually begins
🎣 Hook (first 3 seconds)

Most spiritual traditions have an end point. Gnosis doesn't. The path doesn't end. It deepens.

⚡ Disrupt (the unexpected angle)

After 8 weeks — after orientation, cosmology, texts, Sophia, meditation, Archon work, ritual — Module 8 asks one question: *What do you actually know now?* Not what you've read. Not what you believe. The Gnostic tradition is a living tradition. It didn't die when the Church burned the texts. It continued through alchemy, Kabbalah, Sufi mysticism, and today — in the work of everyone who asks: what am I, really, beneath what I've been told I am?
