In 367 AD, a letter went out from the Bishop of Alexandria.

It listed which texts were permitted — and which were to be destroyed. Someone, somewhere in Upper Egypt, took 52 texts and hid them in a sealed jar in the desert cliffs near Nag Hammadi.

They waited 1,600 years.

In 1945, a farmer digging for fertiliser found them.

What was in those texts? Everything the early Church tried to erase. The secret teachings of Thomas. The Apocryphon of John. The Thunder, Perfect Mind. The Gospel of Philip. The Reality of the Rulers.

Preview Text
Fear. Attachment. Pride. The Gnostics mapped them all 2,000 years ago...
Email Body (copy into MailerLite)
The Gnostics described seven Archons — cosmic rulers who govern the material world and keep souls trapped in unconscious patterns.

For a long time, scholars read this as superstition. Planetary daemons. Pre-scientific mythology.

But look closer.

The seven Archons map precisely to the seven patterns that keep human beings asleep: **fear, attachment, anger, ignorance, envy, greed, pride.** The Gnostics weren't describing external beings — they were describing the architecture of the unconscious mind.

And the path out? Not willpower. Not positive thinking.

*Recognition.* The Greek word is **epinoia** — the capacity to see through the illusion. To look at fear and say: "I see you. You are not me."

That recognition, repeated, is liberation.
